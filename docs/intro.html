My research focuses on observational cosmology, including statistical reconstructions of cosmological observables, gamma-ray bursts as high-redshift probes, and Bayesian and machine-learning–based inference methods. This site collects my ongoing and public research work.

Research & Preprints

Dark Energy & Expansion History

Non-parametric reconstructions of H(z), q(z), and w(z) using Pantheon+, BAO, and cosmic chronometer datasets.

Gaussian Process Modeling

Custom Gaussian Process pipelines for cosmological data analysis with uncertainty propagation and kernel studies.

Gamma-Ray Bursts

Calibration, luminosity correlations, and machine-learning approaches for GRBs as cosmological probes.

Quantum Information & Cosmology

Classical and quantum Fisher information analyses with applications to cosmological parameter inference.
